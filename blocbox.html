Blocbox

Blocbox

SaaS Web Application

Blocbox is a responsive, software as a service (SaaS) web application built for collecting links, documents and images in a single location online, allowing users to collaborate with others easily online.

The Challenge
As the workplace evolves, so should the tools we use

The modern workplace has changed dramatically in the last 10-15 years, as technology continues to shape how we work together at the office and remotely from home. As our work environments evolve, so should the platforms we use to collaborate with each other.

Within our teams at work, we share and save information in different ways, often complicating the tasks at hand and taking more time instead of saving time and accomplishing goals. This disorganization takes time away from real work that needs to be done.

The Solution
Keep it simple

Blocbox provides a simple, centralized way to organize, save and share information with any device across platforms, whether on desktop, tablet, laptop or mobile. By allowing users to group images, create notes and add links organized by projects or teams, Blocbox provides a simple, intuitive way to organize all your work information in a centralized location.

Discovery
Get to know the user

Next, I surveyed a group of users, in order to understand the typical Blocbox target audience and determine their requirements. I surveyed a group of users to understand how they currently save and share information. Data was collected from the multiple-choice and short-answer questions from the surveys. From this small data set, I learned user browsing behaviors, how they save and share content, as well as what apps they use to create or save info online.

During this discovery phase, I also conducted a competitive analysis on current competitors like Dropbox, Evernote, Box and Google Drive. The results and responses are summarized to inform our design process and to find potential patterns.

User Research
Analyzing survey responses

Analyzing the responses received from the surveys we sent out, all own a smart phone, tablet or laptop. The majority of users browse content online with mobile devices, while a little over one-third use a tablet or laptop.

Social Media websites were the most popular content browsed online, with news ranking second. Only a quarter of the applicants surveyed save links or images they find online. Over half save content occasionally. Managing bookmarks and easily grouping content easily will be a focus of our Blocbox dashboard moving forward.

Competitive Analysis
User requirements

To help determine how Blocbox measures up against competition, we took a closer look at our requirements for the web app, comparing them to similar features in competitor’s applications, like Dropbox and Box. This will help us determine how our product is different than those made by Dropbox and Box. More importantly, we can solve measurable usability issues by performing specific tasks, meeting user requirements that will help us stand apart from competitors.
